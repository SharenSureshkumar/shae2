Lions — The King of Beasts

Powerful, social big cats native to Africa and a small region of India. Learn about their behavior, habitat and how people are helping protect them.

Behavior & Hunting

Lions are the most social of big cats. Females typically hunt cooperatively, while males defend territory. They communicate with roars, body language and scent marking.

  • Prides include related females, offspring and a small number of males.
  • Roaring helps coordinate groups and warn rivals.
  • Hunting success depends on stealth, teamwork, and timing.

Habitat

Lions prefer grasslands, savannas and open woodlands. They need sufficient prey and access to water. Human activity has fragmented many populations.

Conservation

Populations have declined due to habitat loss, human-wildlife conflict and illegal hunting. Conservation focuses on habitat protection, community programs and anti-poaching measures.

Support: local conservation NGOs, ethical tourism, and habitat corridors.
